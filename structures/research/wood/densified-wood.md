# Densified Wood Research

Date started: 2026-04-05
Status: Active line of research

## 1) Working Definition
Densified wood is wood that has been mechanically and/or chemically processed to reduce porosity and increase density, typically improving strength, hardness, and wear resistance.

## 2) Why It Matters
- Potential replacement for higher-impact materials in some structural and interior applications.
- Can achieve significant gains in mechanical performance while retaining a wood-based material identity.
- Relevant for architectural uses where durability and tactile quality are both important.

## 3) Core Mechanisms To Understand
- Cell wall collapse and lumen reduction during compression.
- Role of lignin and hemicellulose in post-treatment stability.
- Moisture interactions and how they affect spring-back.
- Influence of thermal treatment and resin/chemical modification on long-term performance.

## 4) Main Densification Pathways
### A. Thermo-mechanical densification
- Heat + pressure to compress wood structure.
- Often simple process chain, but spring-back can be a major issue.

### B. Chemical pretreatment + compression
- Partial delignification or softening steps before compression.
- Can enable higher densification and bonding between cell walls.

### C. Impregnation-assisted densification
- Polymer/resin infiltration before or after compression.
- Improves dimensional stability and moisture resistance, but affects recyclability and material purity.

## 5) Key Performance Metrics
Track and compare across papers/processes:
- Density (kg/m^3)
- Modulus of rupture (MOR)
- Modulus of elasticity (MOE)
- Janka hardness or equivalent indentation hardness
- Impact toughness
- Thickness swelling / water uptake
- Creep and fatigue behavior
- Fire behavior and thermal degradation profile

## 6) Architectural Relevance Questions
- Which densification routes are viable at building-material scale?
- How stable is densified wood under humidity cycling in real interiors?
- Can it be machined/joined with standard timber workflows?
- What finishing systems are compatible without sacrificing breathability?
- What are the cost and energy implications versus engineered wood products?

## 7) Initial Literature Targets
Find and review:
- Foundational papers on wood cell wall densification mechanics.
- Comparative studies: untreated wood vs thermo-mechanical vs chemically assisted densification.
- Long-term durability studies (humidity, UV, biological resistance).
- LCA studies comparing densified wood to steel/aluminum/plastics in specific use cases.

## 8) Risks / Unknowns
- Spring-back and dimensional instability over time.
- Moisture sensitivity if treatment is incomplete.
- Potential brittleness at extreme densification levels.
- Process scalability and consistent quality control.
- Environmental tradeoffs from chemical treatments.
